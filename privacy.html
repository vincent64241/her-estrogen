Privacy Policy + HIPAA Notice

Effective Date: May 13, 2026

1. Protected Health Information

HerEstrogen may collect health related information including:

  • Symptoms
  • Hormonal health questionnaires
  • Medical history
  • Treatment preferences
  • Provider communications

This information may constitute Protected Health Information under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act.

2. How We Collect Information

We collect information when you:

  • Complete online assessments
  • Create an account
  • Submit payment information
  • Communicate with providers
  • Subscribe to communications

3. Information Collected

Personal Information

  • Name
  • Date of birth
  • Address
  • Phone number
  • Email address

Health Information

  • Menopause related symptoms
  • Medical questionnaire responses
  • Treatment goals

Financial Information

Payment processing is handled by Stripe. HerEstrogen does not store full payment card numbers.

4. Use of Information

We may use your information to:

  • Coordinate care
  • Determine eligibility
  • Process payments
  • Provide customer support
  • Improve services
  • Send educational content
  • Send marketing communications with consent

5. Business Associates

HerEstrogen works with HIPAA compliant service providers, including:

  • OpenLoop Health
  • Stripe
  • Licensed pharmacy partners

These providers may access information solely for authorized operational purposes.

6. Patient Rights Under HIPAA

You may request:

  • Access to your records
  • Correction of inaccurate information
  • Restrictions on certain disclosures
  • Confidential communications
  • Accounting of disclosures

Requests may be submitted through our support team.

7. SMS and Marketing Consent

Marketing text messages are sent only with express consent.

You may opt out at any time by replying STOP.

Transactional messages related to your care may still be sent where legally permitted.

8. Data Security

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal and health information, including:

  • Encryption
  • Access controls
  • Role based permissions
  • Secure third party vendors
  • HIPAA compliant workflows
